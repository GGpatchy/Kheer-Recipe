How To Make Kheer?

Introduction
       Kheer is a traditional Indian rice pudding made with milk, rice, sugar, and flavored with cardamom, saffron, and sometimes garnished with nuts and raisins.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup basmati rice (or any other long-grain rice)
  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/4 to 1/3 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods (crushed or powdered)
  • 8-10 saffron strands (optional, soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk)
  • 1-2 tbsp ghee (optional)
  • 10-12 almonds, chopped
  • 10-12 cashews, chopped
  • 10-12 raisins (optional)
  • 2-3 tbsp condensed milk (optional for extra creaminess)
  • 1 tsp rose water (optional for fragrance)

Instructions:

  1. Wash and Soak the Rice:
    • Rinse the rice thoroughly in water until the water runs clear.
    • Soak the rice in water for about 20-30 minutes. Drain and set aside.

  2. Boil the Milk:
    • In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring the milk to a boil on medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent it from burning at the bottom.

  3. Add the Rice:
    • Add the soaked and drained rice to the boiling milk.
    • Lower the heat and cook, stirring continuously to avoid sticking. Let the rice simmer and cook in the milk for about 30-40 minutes, or until the rice is soft and the milk has thickened. Stir every few minutes to avoid scorching.

  4. Add Sugar and Flavors:
    • Once the rice is fully cooked, add the sugar and mix well. Let it dissolve completely.
    • Add the crushed cardamom and saffron strands (along with the soaked milk, if using).

  5. Optional Garnish:
    • In a separate pan, heat 1-2 tbsp ghee and lightly fry the chopped nuts and raisins until golden brown. Add this to the kheer.

  6. Thicken and Serve:
    • Cook for another 5-10 minutes until the kheer reaches the desired consistency. You can add condensed milk at this stage for extra creaminess if desired.
    • Turn off the heat and add a few drops of rose water for fragrance, if using.

  7. Cool and Serve:
    • Kheer can be served hot, warm, or chilled. Garnish with extra nuts and saffron strands before serving.
